Daniel Gomez

As a scientist immersed in the world of medical science I found that the areas of genome biology, scaling multi-omics, and human translational science to be the way to serve my community. I am captivated by the intricate interplay of genes, genomics, and human medical molcular genetics alongside molecule evolution given its potential for advancing patient care through cutting-edge research. Through my work, I hope to unite STEM fields and be a beacon of hope for a joyous, brighter and brilliant future.
As a graduate student, I am currently working on my thesis in the Snyder Lab to complete the California State University, East Bay Master's of Science Degree in Computational Biology with a concentration in Genetics Bioinformatics at Stanford Genetics.

My research aims to advance computational biology and medicine through the development of novel machine learning algorithms and software tools. Prior to graduate school, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Biology: Cell and Molecular Biology from San Francisco State University.
In my free time, I enjoy exploring various forms of art, including music, reading, and writing. I also prioritize my physical and mental well-being through exercise, healthy eating, regular sleep, and meditation practices. Striving for a work-life balance I always find time to unite my families, friends, and myself.

Unraveling the Structural Dynamics of Human Pegivirus-1 RNA-Dependent RNA Polymerase Using Computational Methods
Daniel J. Gomez
ResearchGate, 2022   (Publication)
pdf / Download citation / Presentation

This is the first structural predictions of the Human pegivirus-1 (HPgV-1) using AI techniques, Meta AI ESM-2/ESMfold and ColabFold: AlphaFold 2 using MMseqs2. Also applied PyMol, Coot, and UCSF Chimera for annotation and superposition (superimposed) structures of predictions, and molecular movies.

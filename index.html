Dan Gomez

I am a Senior Researcher at Gomera Research in the Bay Area, where I work on computational immunology and machine learning. I am doing my first masters at Cal State East Bay in Biological Sciences with a concentration in Virophysics and Structural Molecular Biology. You can read more about this in on my About page. I'm planning on doing a second masters in molecular science and software engineering (MSSE) at UC Berkeley soon. In the future, I will be working to complete a PhD in Biological and Medical Informatics (BMI).

I received my Bachelor of Science in Molecular Cell Biology from SFSU , Class of 2022, where I conducted research with Scott Roy and Nicole Salazar, and my research was focused on DNA methylation, alternative splicing, and gene expression bioinformatics (starburst plots, plotly, etc). Additionally, during my undergraduate training at Hawaii, in Dr. Bruce Shiramizu's lab, I discovered a feedback and feedforward loop molecular circuit in the human body that triggered neuroinflammation and subsequently discovered a novel theraputic target for HIV-1-associated neurocognitive disorder (HAND). I designed experiments that were funded by university grants (UROP) to examine the proteomics of CSF fluid and the transcriptomics of necropsy brain tissue for this proof-of-concept study. After immunoassays, I volunteered in Hemal Patel's Cardiac & Neuro Protection Laboratories. Here, I was able to learn about membrane/lipid rafts (MLR), cardioprotective trafficking via caveola (caveolins) routing to the mitochondrai through Gi-protein dependency. I learned how to conduct in vitro assays alongside ex vivo whole organ (heart) excisions and cannulations onto an isolated heart stand (Langendorff Apparatus). In 2020, I received an award to join the DiVERGE Program, which allowed me to arrive at UF and join Dr. Chris Vulpe's Physiology Lab. I was able to assist in research with a PhD candidate who had projects in high-throughput toxicitiy modesl, mitotoxicity (Mitotox), metabolomics, and biomonitoring.

At Gomera I work on the Gomera App, AI-assistance for robotic surgery and Surgical Data Science.

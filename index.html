Dan Gomez

I am a Director of Multiomics in the Multiomics Structural Biology and Network Physiology Division at Gomera Research. We are building out this division/department to have an industry leading functional Neuro-Spatial Multi-Omics Structural Biology (NSMOSB) unit. As the Lead Molecular Research Scientist and Biomedical AI/ML Engineer at Gomera in the Bay Area, I work on spatial multi-omics, quantum computational immunology and machine learning. In parralell, I am building out a multi-dimensional healthcare and biotechnology company, GomeBio, that will provide neuroimages and bioimages, genetic testings, and individualized medicine. At GomeBio, I am the Head of Neuroinformatics and Clinical Biomarkers Program. I will build a well curated team to meet our requirements and goals.

At Gomera, as a MedTech company, we identify cures, advance diagnostics, and deliver effective therapeutics to attenuate the global death toll from life-threating diseases/conditions. To do this, I am the facilitating channel partnerships and working with investors. I also monitor the development of the Gomera App, AI-assistance for robotic surgery, Surgical Data Science, and oversee the direction of the company as a whole.

While finishing my degree, I took classes in Cancer Biology, Genetics, Honors Genetics, Biochemistry I & II, General Virology, Physics, and Experiments in Cell and Molecular Biology that prepared me for graduate studies. I received my Bachelor of Science in Molecuar Cell Biology from SFSU, Class of 2022, where I conducted research with Scott Roy and Nicole Salazar. This research with Dr. Salazar was successful and she continues to study chemokines in cancer. Since getting my degree, I instantly aligned with the largest biotechnology company in the world Roche Diagnostics. I was working in Department of Antibody Formulations of Roche Tissue Diagnostics (RTD). I desired to do my own research and joined an active research lab at Drexel University College of Medicine in the Department of Microbiology & Immunology. We focused on dendritic cells (DCs) and then explored neurological infections which I had been researching for a decade at this point. I co-first authored a publication in co-infection and cancer. This review paper has been cited four times as of March 2023 and one citation was for a PhD student to obtain their doctorate degree in their dissertation. Other articles are building on our research to tell a story about microbes and their impact on cancer. I kept researching and publishing in cancer as I got started in a MS program at CSUEB. I continue to write, take graduate classes, and review and edit research publications in the scientific and medical community. I have been invited to speak and give presentations in conferences around the world.

Daniel Gomez

As a scientist immersed in the world of biomedical science I found that the areas of precision bioinformatics, exercise multi-omics, computational biology, and human translational science to be the way to serve my community. I am captivated by the intricate interplay of genes, genomics, and human genetics alongside moleculary evolution given its potential for advancing medicine, patient care, and human performance through cutting-edge research. Through my work, I hope to unite STEM fields and be a beacon of hope for a joyous, brighter and brilliant future.
As a graduate student, I am currently working on my MS thesis in the Snyder Lab at Stanford Genetics to complete the Master's of Science Degree in Biology at California State University, East Bay. My research is in precision exercise medicine, biomedical data science and computational systems biology, as well as data analysis.
Specifically, creating an exerkine (exercise + cytokines) map.

Prior to graduate school, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Biology: Cell and Molecular Biology from San Francisco State University.
In my free time, I enjoy exploring various forms of art, including music, reading, and writing. Striving for a work-life balance, I also prioritize my physical and mental well-being through vigorous exercise, healthy eating, regular sleep, and mindfulness practices.
